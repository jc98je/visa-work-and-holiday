📖 Reglas de Gramatica

📌 Present Simple

Se usa para acciones habituales y hechos generales.

Afirmativa: I/You/We/They work | He/She/It works

Negativa: I/You/We/They don't work | He/She/It doesn't work

Interrogativa: Do I/you/we/they work? | Does he/she/it work?

Ejemplo: She works in an office. / They don't live in Sydney.

📌 Past Simple (Regular)

Se usa para acciones terminadas en el pasado.

Afirmativa: I/You/We/They worked | He/She/It worked

Negativa: I/You/We/They didn't work | He/She/It didn't work

Interrogativa: Did I/you/we/they work?

Reglas: Verbo + ed (work→worked) | Termina en e: +d (live→lived) | Consonante+y: cambia y por ied (study→studied)

Ejemplo: I visited Sydney last year. / She didn't go to school yesterday.

📌 Past Simple (Irregulares)

Verbos que cambian completamente en pasado:

go→went | have→had | eat→ate | see→saw | come→came

take→took | give→gave | know→knew | think→thought | buy→bought

write→wrote | read→read | speak→spoke | run→ran | make→made

Ejemplo: I went to the beach. / She ate lunch at noon.

📌 Future (will / going to)

Will → decision espontanea, predicciones, promesas

Going to → planes ya decididos, intenciones

Will: I/You/We/They will work | He/She/It will work

Going to: I am going to work | She is going to work

Negativa: I won't work (will not) | I am not going to work

Ejemplo: It will rain tomorrow. / I'm going to study English.

📌 Modals (can / should / must)

Can → habilidad, permiso, posibilidad

Should → consejo, recomendacion

Must → obligacion, necesidad fuerte

Can: I/You/We/They can work | He/She/It can work (sin -s)

Should: I should study more.

Must: You must wear a helmet.

Negativa: can't / shouldn't / mustn't (prohibicion)

Ejemplo: I can swim. / You should exercise. / You must not smoke here.

📌 Comparativos y Superlativos

Adjetivo corto (1 silaba): + er / + est → tall→taller→tallest

Adjetivo largo (2+ silabas): + more / + most → beautiful→more beautiful→most beautiful

Irregulares: good→better→best | bad→worse→worst | far→farther→farthest

Comparativo: A is taller than B. | A is more expensive than B.

Superlativo: A is the tallest in the class. | A is the most beautiful city.

Ejemplo: Sydney is bigger than Perth. / This is the best restaurant.

📌 Plurales

Regla general: + s → book → books

Termina en s, sh, ch, x, z: + es → bus → buses

Termina en consonante + y: cambia y por ies → city → cities

Termina en vocal + y: + s → boy → boys

Termina en f/fe: cambia por ves → knife → knives

Irregulares: man→men, woman→women, child→children, foot→feet, tooth→teeth, mouse→mice

📌 Articulos (a/an/the)

A → antes de consonante: a book, a house

An → antes de vocal: an apple, an office

The → algo especifico/unico: the sun, the book on the table

Sin articulo → nombres propios, generalidades: Lima, water is important

Ejemplo: I want a coffee. / She works in an office. / Pass me the salt.

📌 Preposiciones (in/on/at)

IN → meses, anios, partes del dia, lugares cerrados: in July, in 2026, in the morning, in the room

ON → dias, fechas, superficies: on Monday, on July 15th, on the table

AT → horas, lugares especificos, eventos: at 3pm, at home, at school, at the party

Ejemplo: I wake up at 7am in the morning. / The meeting is on Monday.

📌 There is / There are

There is + sustantivo singular: There is a book on the table.

There are + sustantivo plural: There are many books on the table.

Negativa: There isn't / There aren't

Interrogativa: Is there a book? / Are there any books?

Ejemplo: There is a cat in the garden. / There are 20 students in the class.

📌 Question Words (Wh-questions)

What → que: What is your name?

Where → donde: Where do you live?

When → cuando: When is the meeting?

Who → quien: Who is your teacher?

Why → por que: Why are you late?

How → como: How are you? / How much is this?

How many → cuantos (contable): How many brothers do you have?

How much → cuanto (incontable/precio): How much does it cost?
