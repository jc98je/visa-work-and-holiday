📐 Gramática Básica

Presente Simple +

Uso: Hábitos, rutinas, hechos generales.

Estructura: Sujeto + verbo (s/es en 3ra persona)

✅ I work from home. ✅ She plays tennis. ✅ The train leaves at 8am.

Adverbios de frecuencia: always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never

Posición: Antes del verbo principal, después de "be"

✅ I always wake up early. ✅ She is never late.

✏️ Ejercicio — Completa con presente simple:

1. She in a bank.

2. I breakfast at 7am.

3. He tennis every weekend.

4. The bus at 9am.

5. She like coffee. (negativo)

Pasado Simple +

Uso: Acciones terminadas en el pasado con momento definido.

Estructura: Sujeto + verbo pasado (-ed o irregular)

✅ I visited Cusco last year. ✅ She went to the bank yesterday. ✅ We watched a movie.

Futuro (Going to / Will) +

Going to: Planes e intenciones futuras

I am going to study English this weekend. (plan)

Will: Decisiones espontáneas, predicciones

I will help you with that. (decisión espontánea)

¿Cuándo usar cada uno?

Plan: "I am going to travel to Australia next year."
Decisión: "The phone is ringing — I will answer it."

A/An: indefinite (a book, an apple)

The: definite (the book on the table)

Plural: book→books, child→children, man→men, woman→women

I need a pen. The pen is on the table. I have two children.

Present Perfect vs Past Simple +
Present PerfectPast Simple
I have visited Australia. (experiencia)I visited Australia in 2023. (momento)
She has lived here for 3 years. (duración)She lived in Lima for 2 years. (terminó)
Regla: Present Perfect = sin momento definido. Past Simple = con momento definido (yesterday, last week, in 2020).

✍️ Writing — Task 1

Objetivo

Describe gráficos objetivamente — sin opinión.

Lenguaje útil
  • Tendencias: rise, increase, fall, decline
  • Velocidad: sharply, steadily, gradually
  • Comparación: compared to, whereas
Reported Speech (Estilo Indirecto) +

Estructura: Sujeto + verbo reporting + (pronombre) + frase (cambiando tiempo/pronombre)

Direct SpeechReported Speech
"I am tired," she said.She said she was tired.
"I have finished," he said.He said he had finished.
"I will call you," she said.She said she would call me.
"I can help," he said.He said he could help.
"I must go," she said.She said she had to go.
"I went home," he said.He said he had gone home.

Verbos de reporting comunes: said, told, asked, explained, replied, mentioned, suggested

Cambios de pronombre: I→she, you→I/he, we→they, my→her, here→there, now→then, today→that day, tomorrow→the next day

Direct: "I will visit you tomorrow," she said.
Reported: She said she would visit me the next day.

🗣️ Discussion Practice (Part 3) +

Discussion questions:

Q1: Do you think technology has made people more isolated? +
Model answer: "To some extent, yes. While technology has made communication more convenient, it could be argued that it has also reduced face-to-face interaction, especially among younger generations. That said, it really depends on how people choose to use it. It is often argued that social media creates a false sense of connection. However, a compelling case can be made that technology also enables people to maintain relationships across distances."

Key expressions used: to some extent, it could be argued that, that said, it is often argued that, a compelling case

Q2: How has globalisation affected local cultures? +
Model answer: "Globalisation has had both positive and negative effects on local cultures. On the one hand, it is widely believed that cultural exchange enriches societies. On the other hand, there is a growing concern that local traditions are being eroded by dominant cultures. In light of recent trends, I think governments should take into account the need to preserve cultural heritage while embracing progress."

Key expressions used: it is widely believed that, there is a growing concern, in light of, take into account

Q3: Should education be free for everyone? +
Model answer: "It is often argued that education is a fundamental right and should be accessible to all. I largely agree with this view because the merits of universal education outweigh the costs. In my opinion, when people have access to quality education, they can contribute more effectively to society. However, to what extent this is feasible depends on each country's economic situation."

Key expressions used: it is often argued that, the merits outweigh, in my opinion, to what extent

🎯 Writing Task 2 — Model Essay +

Topic: Some people believe that technology has made our lives more complex. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

Model essay:

It is often argued that modern technology has made our lives unnecessarily complex. While I acknowledge that technology can be overwhelming, I believe its benefits far outweigh its drawbacks.

On the one hand, there is a growing concern that technology creates dependency. Many people feel they cannot function without their smartphones, and the constant need to update software and learn new systems can be frustrating. Furthermore, digital divide means that not everyone has equal access to technology, which can deepen existing inequalities.

On the other hand, it is widely believed that technology has improved quality of life in numerous ways. For instance, artificial intelligence is transforming healthcare, enabling earlier diagnosis of diseases. Moreover, renewable energy technology offers solutions to climate change. What matters most is how we adapt to these changes.

In conclusion, while technology presents challenges, the merits outweigh the drawbacks. We must take into account that progress is inevitable, and focus on ensuring that everyone can benefit from it.

1. modern technology has made our lives complex.

2. that technology creates dependency.

3. technology has improved quality of life.

4. We must that progress is inevitable.
