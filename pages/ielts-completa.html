📐 Gramática Básica

⏰ Present Simple +

Uso: Acciones habituales y hechos generales.

TipoEstructuraEjemplo
AfirmativaI/You/We/They + verbo | He/She/It + verbosI work. She works.
Negativadon't/doesn't + verboI don't work. She doesn't work.
InterrogativaDo/Does + sujeto + verbo?Do you work? Does she work?
She works in an office. / They don't live in Sydney.

💡 Tip: IELTS 4.5 solo necesitas usar bien present simple y past simple.

⏪ Past Simple +

Uso: Acciones terminadas en el pasado.

RegularIrregular
work → workedgo → went
live → livedhave → had
play → playedsee → saw
study → studiedeat → ate
I visited Sydney last year. / She ate lunch at noon.
⏩ Future (will / going to) +

Will: Decisiones espontáneas, predicciones, promesas

Going to: Planes ya decididos, intenciones

It will rain tomorrow. / I'm going to study English.
⚖️ Comparativos y Superlativos +
Adjetivo cortoAdjetivo largoIrregulares
tall → taller → tallestbeautiful → more beautiful → most beautifulgood → better → best
big → bigger → biggestexpensive → more expensivebad → worse → worst
Sydney is bigger than Perth. / This is the best restaurant.
📌 Artículos (a/an/the) +

A → antes de consonante: a book, a house

An → antes de vocal: an apple, an office

The → algo específico/único: the sun, the book on the table

Sin artículo → nombres propios, generalidades: Lima, water is important

I want a coffee. / She works in an office.
📍 Preposiciones (in/on/at) +
INONAT
meses, años, partes del díadías, fechas, superficieshoras, lugares específicos
in July, in 2026, in the morningon Monday, on July 15th, on the tableat 3pm, at home, at school
I wake up at 7am in the morning. / The meeting is on Monday.

📐 Gramática Intermedia

⏳ Present Perfect +

Uso: Experiencias sin tiempo definido / acción que empezó en el pasado y sigue ahora.

TipoEstructuraEjemplo
Afirmativahave/has + participioI have visited Sydney.
Negativahaven't/hasn't + participioI haven't been to Perth.
InterrogativaHave/Has + sujeto + participio?Have you worked in Australia?
I have lived in Lima for 5 years. (vivo aquí desde hace 5 años)

💡 Tip: Usa con "for" (duración) y "since" (punto de partida).

🔄 Present Perfect Continuous +

Uso: Énfasis en la duración de una acción que empezó en el pasado y continúa.

I have been studying English for 3 months. / She has been working all day.

Diferencia: "I've read that book" (terminé) vs "I've been reading" (todavía lo leo).

🔮 First Conditional +

Uso: Situaciones futuras reales/posibles.

If it rains, I will take an umbrella. / If you study hard, you will pass.

⚠️ Importante: NO uses "will" después de "if".

💭 Second Conditional +

Uso: Hipotético presente/futuro improbable.

If I had more money, I would travel the world. / If I were you, I would accept the job.
📝 Passives (Voz Pasiva) +

Uso: Cuando la acción importa más que quién la realiza.

TiempoEstructuraEjemplo
Present Simpleam/is/are + participioEnglish is spoken here.
Past Simplewas/were + participioThe house was built in 2010.
Present Perfecthas/have been + participioThe report has been finished.
Futurewill be + participioThe results will be announced.
📊 Reported Speech +

Uso: Reportar lo que otra persona dijo.

DirectoIndirecto
"I am tired."She said she was tired.
"I will come."He said he would come.
"I have finished."She said she had finished.
"I'm working hard." → She said she was working hard.

📐 Gramática Avanzada

💭 Third Conditional +

Uso: Hipotético pasado (cosas que NO pasaron).

If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam.
(No estudié lo suficiente → no aprobé)
🔄 Mixed Conditionals +

Uso: Combinar pasado y presente.

If I had taken that job, I would be living in Sydney now.
(Pasado diferente → presente diferente)
📊 Relative Clauses +

Uso: Añadir información sin crear frases nuevas.

RelativeUsoEjemplo
who/thatPersonas (sujeto)The man who called is my boss.
which/thatCosasThe book which I read was great.
whosePosesiónThe girl whose phone rang...
whereLugaresThe city where I grew up...
whenTiempoSummer is the time when I relax.
✂️ Participle Clauses +

Uso: Formas compactas de cláusulas (Writing avanzado).

Living in a big city, she enjoys the nightlife. = Because she lives in a big city...
Exhausted from work, he went to bed early. = Because he was exhausted...
🎭 Cleft Sentences +

Uso: Enfatizar diferentes partes de la frase (Writing y Speaking Band 6+).

It was yesterday that I arrived. (enfasis en cuándo)
What I need is more time. (enfasis en qué)

🗣️ Speaking — Conversación oral

La entrevista dura 11-14 minutos y tiene 3 partes. Para tu visa, necesitas comunicarte en situaciones cotidianas.

Part 1 — Preguntas personales (4-5 min)

Temas típicos: Trabajo, estudios, hobbies, familia, rutina diaria.

Estrategia
  • Respuestas de 2-4 oraciones
  • Añade siempre una razón o ejemplo
  • No respondas solo "Yes" o "No"
Pregunta: "What do you do for work?"
Respuesta: "I'm a waiter at a restaurant in Lima. I've been working there for two years, and I really enjoy meeting new people from different countries."

Part 2 — Long Turn / Cue Card (3-4 min)

1 minuto de preparación + 1-2 minutos hablando sobre un tema.

Cómo usar tu minuto de preparación
  • Anota 3-4 palabras clave por cada punto
  • No escribas oraciones completas
Cue card ejemplo: "Describe a place you would like to visit in Australia. You should say: where it is, why you want to go there, what you would do there, and explain why it interests you."

Part 3 — Discusión (4-5 min)

Preguntas más abstractas sobre el tema de Part 2.

Estructura de respuesta
  • Opinión directa → Razón → Ejemplo
Pregunta: "Why do people like to travel?"
Respuesta: "I think people enjoy travelling because it allows them to experience different cultures and escape their daily routine. For example, when I visit a new place, I feel more relaxed and open-minded."
